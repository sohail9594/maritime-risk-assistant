SOURCE: International Telecommunication Union
SUBJECT: Fiber Optic Bend Radius and Tension
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Handling Physics Guideline
DETAILS: High-voltage, deep-sea fiber optic cables are transported on massive, multi-ton rotational carousels. If these carousels are subjected to sudden braking forces during maritime transit (e.g., a ship dropping anchor at emergency speeds), the kinetic tension can exceed the cable's "minimum bend radius." This causes the internal glass fibers to micro-fracture. Spools subjected to emergency kinetic stops must undergo full optical-reflectometry testing before installation.
