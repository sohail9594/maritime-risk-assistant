SOURCE: International Maritime Organization (IMO) - Welfare Division
SUBJECT: Emergency Berthing for Livestock Carriers
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Humanitarian Maritime Law
DETAILS: Livestock carriers transporting live animals operate under strict humanitarian maritime laws. If a vessel faces route diversions or blockades that reduce its onboard fodder and fresh water supplies to below a 3-day reserve, the captain must invoke the "Safe Haven" protocol. Under this protocol, the vessel is legally mandated to dock at the nearest physical deep-water port to replenish life-support provisions, overriding all commercial schedules, port congestion queues, and standard diplomatic blockades.
