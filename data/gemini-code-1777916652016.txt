SOURCE: International Maritime Organization (IMO)
SUBJECT: Dynamic Diversion of In-Transit Vessels
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Legal Advisory
DETAILS: Under emergency maritime law, commercial vessels currently underway in the Arabian Sea that are subjected to sudden carrier suspensions (e.g., Maersk Gulf Ban) are legally permitted to divert to the nearest safe harbor without voiding their charter agreements. For vessels currently off the coast of Oman, diverting to the Port of Salalah is legally designated as the "Optimal Safe Haven," overriding initial instructions to proceed to Jebel Ali or return to India.
