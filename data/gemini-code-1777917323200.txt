SOURCE: Global Agricultural Freight Standards
SUBJECT: Mixed-Load Fruit Ripening Hazard
DOCUMENT_TYPE: Biochemical Guideline
DETAILS: When consolidating agricultural shipments due to port delays, logistics planners must account for Ethylene gas production. "Climacteric" fruits (like bananas, avocados, and tomatoes) release large amounts of Ethylene gas as they ripen. If these are packed in the same poorly ventilated hold or transshipment warehouse as Ethylene-sensitive crops (like leafy greens, carrots, or watermelons), the sensitive crops will rapidly yellow, decay, and become unsellable within 72 hours.
